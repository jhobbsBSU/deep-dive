The life of a penguin isn't easy

Penguins are sadly one of the most threatened groups of seabirds, with half of the 18 species listed by Birdlife as either Vulnerable or Endangered.

Avoiding predators daily

Penguins have predators both on land and in the water. In the water, penguins may become prey to leopard seals, fur seals, sea lions, sharks, or killer whales.

and staring death in the face

Gentoo penguins can reach 22 miles per hour, around the maximum speed of a Great White Shark!

Plastic pollutes their home

Plastic found in our oceans comes mostly from land based sources (80%), with only 20% from marine sources.

Posing a threat to to all

Approximately 36% of seabird species have been found entangled in plastic litter.

Hunting has become hard due to overfishing and climate change

Since 1970, krill populations have declined by 80% in the Southern Ocean around Antarctica due to reductions in sea ice from climate change.

And they are not always successful

Overfishing of anchovetta (a small fish), the primary food source of the Humboldt penguin, has contributed to penguin population declines.
